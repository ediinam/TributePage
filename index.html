    Timeline
  • March 30, 1945 - Born in Ripley, Surrey, England
  • 1961 - Began studying at the Kingston College of Art on a one-year probation and was expelled for lack of progress as he had not submitted enough work.
  • 1963 - Joins his first band, The Roosters
  • October 1963 - Keith Relf and Paul Samwell-Smith recruited him to become a member of The Yardbirds. During his 18-month tenure with The Yardbirds, he earnes his nickname, Slowhand, and recorded his first albums: Five Live Yardbirds and Sonny Boy Williamson and The Yardbirds.
  • April 1965 - John Mayall invited Eric to join his band, John Mayal's Bluesbreakers. With this group, Clapton earned his second name: "God" which came from an admirer's graffiti on the wall of London's Islington Tube Station.
  • July 1966 - Eric teamed up with Jack Bruce and Ginger Baker to form Cream.
  • November 1968 - The Cream disbanded. Clapton formed Blind Faith with Steve Winwood, Ginger Baker and Rick Grech.
  • 1970 - Eric forms Derek and the Dominos with Jim Gordon, Carl Radle and Bobby Whitlock. The band recorded the seminal rock album, Layla and Other Assorted Love Songs, after which they drifted apart.
  • January 1973 - Re-launches his career with two concerts at London's Rainbow Theater after a three year struggle with heroin addiction.
  • 1974 - Reappeared with a new style and sound with 461 Ocean Boulevard.
  • March 1979 - Eric marries his first wife, Pattie Boyd Harrison. The couple divorced in 1989.
  • January 1982 - Eric enters the Hazelden Foundation, a rehabilitation facility in the United States for addiction to alcohol.
  • 1992 - Releases Unplugged and the Grammy winning single, "Tears In Heaven" which was written after the death of his son, Conor.
  • 1994 - Releases From The Cradle. The album was Clapton's tribute to his musical heroes and contained cover versions of blues classinc.
  • February 1998 - Announces the opening of Crossroads Centre, a rehabilitation facility for drug and alcohol abuse on the island of Antigua.
  • 1998 - Releases soul-influenced Pilgrim.
  • June 1999 - Clapton auctioned 100 of his guitars for the foundation which was established to provide care for some of the poorest people of the Caribbean. It raised $5 million. Few days later Eric hosted a concert to benefit the Centre at New York City's Madison Square Garden.
  • 2000 - Records album with American blues legend, B. B. King, Riding With The King.
  • June 2004 - Eric hosted the First Crossroads Guitar Festival in Dallas, Texas, which was the second and final major fundraising effort for the Centre. Additionally, a second guitar action took place on 24 June 2004. It raised $6 million.
  • 2005 - Re-formes Cream with Ginger Baker and Jack Bruce.
  • October 2007 - Eric's autobiography, Clapton, was published.
